ADVISORY BOARD

The Witness Protection Advisory Board is established under Section 3P of the Witness Protection Act

The Witness Protection Advisory Board is an unincorporated body whose role is to advise the Agency generally on the exercise of its powers and the performance of its functions under the Act, including:

  • Advise on the formulation of witness protection policies in accordance with the current law and international best practices
  • Have general oversight on the administration of the Agency
  • Approve the budgetary estimates of the Agency
  • Perform any other functions as may be conferred by this Act or any other law.

It is comprised of the major stakeholders in Witness Protection including:

  • The Attorney General as Chairman
  • The Cabinet Secretary responsible for matters relating to Finance
  • The Director-General, National Intelligence Service
  • The Inspector-General of Police
  • The Commissioner General of Prisons
  • The Director of Public Prosecutions
  • The Chairperson on the Kenya National Commission on Human Rights

The Director of the Witness Protection Agency shall be the Secretary of the Board.

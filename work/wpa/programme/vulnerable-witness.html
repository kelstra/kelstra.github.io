VULNERABLE WITNESS

In performing its functions under the Witness Protection Act, the Agency is mandated to give due regard to the particular needs of vulnerable witnesses. Vulnerable witnesses include:

  • Minors / Children
  • Victims of sexual and gender-based violence
  • Elderly or ill persons
  • Persons declared vulnerable witnesses due to personal characteristics
  • Witnesses to certain types of offences
  • Witnesses with close relationship to the perpetrator
  • Witnesses in other circumstances as may be specified

To this effect, the Agency will assign, as appropriate, a support person to assist the vulnerable witness through all the stages of the Witness Protection programme.

MINORS / CHILDREN

With regards to the admission or inclusion of a minor into the Witness Protection Programme, it should be noted that such applications can be done on behalf of the minor by any of the following:

  • Any witness and/or related person
  • An intermediary
  • Legal representative
  • Parent or legal guardian
  • Public prosecutor
  • Law enforcement agency

It is important to also note that an application for the protection of a minor can be made by a law enforcement agency without the consent of the minor’s parent or legal guardian.  This application may be made without the consent of the minor’s parents or legal guardian if the applicant and the relevant investigating official and/or Public Prosecutor can prove to the Director that exceptional circumstances exist that warrant such an application. Where such an application is made for the inclusion of a minor to the programme without the consent of the minor’s parents or legal guardian, the Director of the Agency shall determine if it is necessary for the minor’s safety that they be included in the programme subsequent to making an application to the High Court for a protection order that will allow the Agency to assume care over the child.
